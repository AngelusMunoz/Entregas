Welcome, young wizard! You have found your way to the most extraordinary place in the wizarding world. This page is your guide to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry — the legendary Scottish castle where witches and wizards aged eleven and above are trained in the magical arts.

Founded over a thousand years ago by four of the greatest wizards of the age, Hogwarts is divided into four Houses, each bearing the name and qualities of its founder. Every student is sorted by the enchanted Sorting Hat into one of these Houses: Gryffindor, Hufflepuff, Ravenclaw, or Slytherin. Explore each house and discover where your magic truly belongs.

✦ A Thousand Years of Magic ✦

—— ✧ ——

The Founding

In the early tenth century, four wizards of unparalleled skill and vision came together to create what would become the finest school of witchcraft and wizardry in the world. Godric Gryffindor, Helga Hufflepuff, Rowena Ravenclaw, and Salazar Slytherin built Hogwarts in the remote Highlands of Scotland, hidden from Muggle eyes by powerful enchantments that persist to this day.

The Castle Grounds

The castle itself is a marvel of magical architecture, with moving staircases, secret passages, and rooms that appear only to those in need. The grounds extend far beyond the castle walls, encompassing the Forbidden Forest (home to centaurs, unicorns, and darker creatures), the Black Lake (inhabited by merpeople and a giant squid), and the Quidditch pitch where students compete in the wizarding world's most beloved sport.

Education & Curriculum

Students at Hogwarts follow a rigorous seven-year program of magical education. Core subjects include Transfiguration, Charms, Potions, Defense Against the Dark Arts, Astronomy, Herbology, and History of Magic. In their third year, students may choose from elective courses such as Divination, Care of Magical Creatures, Arithmancy, and Ancient Runes. The culmination of their education comes in the form of O.W.L.s (Ordinary Wizarding Levels) in fifth year and N.E.W.T.s (Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Tests) in seventh year.

Traditions & Events

Hogwarts is rich in tradition. The school year begins with the Sorting Ceremony, where first-years are placed into their houses by the ancient Sorting Hat. Throughout the year, students compete for the House Cup, earning or losing points based on their academic achievements and behavior. Special events include the Halloween Feast, the Yule Ball (held during the Triwizard Tournament), and the year-end feast where the House Cup is awarded.

The Four Founders

—— ⚡ ——
Godric Gryffindor
The bravest of the four, Gryffindor valued courage and chivalry above all else. His sword is one of Hogwarts' greatest treasures.
Rowena Ravenclaw
The most intelligent founder, Ravenclaw believed that wit and learning were paramount. Her diadem was lost for centuries.
Salazar Slytherin
The most cunning and ambitious, Slytherin valued pure-blood heritage. He secretly built the Chamber of Secrets.
Helga Hufflepuff
The most just and loyal, Hufflepuff believed in hard work and fair play. She was the most inclusive of the founders.

The Four Houses of Hogwarts

— ✦ —

Upon arrival at Hogwarts, every first-year student participates in the Sorting Ceremony, a time-honored tradition dating back to the school's founding. The enchanted Sorting Hat, which once belonged to Godric Gryffindor himself, is placed upon each student's head and determines which of the four houses they will join for their seven years at the school.

Each house has its own unique identity, values, and history. While the Hat primarily considers a student's innate qualities and potential, it also takes into account their preferences and desires. The houses compete throughout the year for the House Cup, earning or losing points based on academic achievement, rule-following, and demonstrations of house values.

Your house becomes your family at Hogwarts. You'll share a common room, dormitories, and many classes with your fellow house members. The bonds formed within your house often last a lifetime, creating friendships and connections that extend far beyond your school years. "Brave at Heart"

Gryffindor values bravery, nerve, chivalry, and daring. Founded by the bold Godric Gryffindor, this house is represented by a lion and its colors are scarlet red and gold. The ghost of Gryffindor is Nearly Headless Nick.
